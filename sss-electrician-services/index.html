    Frequently Asked Questions

    Why are my lights occasionally flickering?

    Flickering lights can indicate a loose electrical connection in your service panel, a failing circuit breaker, or a utility power supply issue. Call VoltPro to schedule a panel safety inspection.

    What is a GFCI outlet and why do I need them?

    Ground Fault Circuit Interrupters are special safety outlets designed to cut off electrical power instantly if a ground fault is detected. NEC code requires them near water sources like kitchens and bathrooms.

    Can my existing electrical panel support an EV charger?

    Most modern 150 or 200-amp electrical panels can support a Level 2 EV charger. Older 60 or 100-amp panels usually require a service panel upgrade first.

    Do you offer 24/7 emergency electrical service?

    Yes! If you experience burning smells from outlets, crackling sounds from your breaker box, or a sudden partial power outage, call us immediately at any hour.

    Do you install Tesla and EV chargers?

    Yes! We are certified installers for Tesla Wall Connectors, ChargePoint, and all major Level 2 EVSE brands. We handle the permit, wiring, and activation.

    How do I know if my panel needs upgrading?

    Signs include frequently tripping breakers, flickering lights, burning smells near the panel, or a panel rated below 200 amps in a modern home.

    Are your electricians licensed and insured?

    Absolutely. All our technicians hold state master electrician licenses, carry $1M liability insurance, and pass drug screening and background checks.
